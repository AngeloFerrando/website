Angelo Ferrando is a Research Fellow at the University of Genova (IT) and an Honorary Research Associate at the University of Liverpool (UK).
Since 2021, he is the coordinator of the CINI "Smart Cities & Communities" node of the University of Genova.
Previously he was a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Manchester (UK) from July 2020 to February 2021, and Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Liverpool (UK) from 2018 to 2020. He was part of the Autonomy and Verification Network at University of Manchester, where he worked on applying RV in the RAIN and ORCA research hubs founded by UKRI.
His main research interests are Formal Methods, Software Engineering, Multi-Agent Systems and Runtime Verification; the latter especially when applied to improve the reliability of intelligent systems.
He is a member of the program committee in several international conferences such as PRIMA, AAMAS and IJCAI.

  • MAIN INTERESTS:
  • Multi-Agent Systems (MASs)
  • Decentralized Runtime Verification (DRV)
  • Static Verification (Model Checking)
